Software Art



Gravity: Emergent Beauty

G:BE is a software art project That I've been developing since 2013, when I started programming.
It grew organically from explorations of these particle systems which can generate such intricate
and appealing imagery from such a simple set of rules. In 2015 it was selected for the University
Circuit of the Curitiba International Biennial.(There's not much information about the
University Circuit, or CUBIC, online, but my name does appear on the Biennial's website here).



For the Biennial I displayed the software running in real time, generating random new images,
as well as the code on a long scroll of paper. All of this was to emphasize the virtual nature
of the project and to demystify the computer as an art tool. For the still pieces, seen here,
the process involved long periods of observation and free-association, something like a
'cloudwatching' method, witnessing beauty arise out of the simple and elegant mathematics of
newtonian physics.
